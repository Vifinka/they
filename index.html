Hello human! And welcome to your guide to using singular They/Them pronouns. Most of us already use the singular they pronoun without even realising it. When we find someone's wallet, we say "Oh no! Someone dropped their wallet. They must be worried sick!" We use the neutral pronoun when we don't know the person's gender and don't want to assume it, or when the person is nonbinary and requests the usage of singular they/them pronouns. Whatever reason you have for being here, it's good that you are educating yourself and expanting your knowledge in the english language.

! Important note !
We use the plural version of the verb BE when using the they pronoun. While this may be strange at first, remember that we also use the plural version of the verb BE when referring to a singular person using the pronoun you
Gender neutral language
While it is rather simple to start using pronouns and quickly adapt to them, there still remains the fact of other words having an intrinsic gender meaning, such as mother, mister, ladies and gentlemen and so on. In this segment we wll look at some of the gender neutral counterparts to the words you might be used to. Feel free to add them to your vocabulary and daily usage:

Mx., M. <==> Mr., Ms., Mrs.

Sibling/Sib <==> Brother/Bro, Sister/Sis

Nibling <==> Nephew, Neice

Grandparent/Grandy/Grangran <==> Grandmother, Grandfather

Kid, Child, Offspring, Descendant, Heir <==> Son, Daughter

Kid, Child, Enby <==> Girl, Boy

Spouse, Partner <==> Wife, Husband

Datemate <==> Boyfriend, Girlfriend

Parent/Par <==> Mother/Mom, Father/Dad

Fan <==> Fangirl, Fanboy

Esteemed/Distinguished/Honoured guests <==> Ladies and Gentlemen

Person, Human <==> Man, Woman

Pals, Folks, Mates, Friends, Everyone, Y'all [You all], Pupils <==> Guys [While guys is used as gender neutral, it also has the conotation of just referring to a group of men. You can use guys, but it's preferable to use the other terms]
